Sandra's Story

Who am I? I am Sandra Smith, and I am a boutique photographer, not like any other photographer.

Aside from photography, I am a wife, mother and dog owner.

I am passionate about the beauty of life, people, the world, art, history, everything.....

I have been described as a "dreamer", I call those goals! As a young girl growing up, I was often told, you can't do that, or that's not for you. I am glad that I didnt listen to that or else I would have missed out on a lot of things, especially photography.

Here's how photography came about....its quite romantic, in fact. I was always interested in it, I have always been attracted to art. I grew up around it. I took up oil painting lessons at the age of 8, while growing up in Hawaii.

I always wanted to study photography, but just didnt know where to look. My local college counselor told me that was not a career and advised me to go into accounting. So, I put this "dream" aside. Then years later, married in and in California, a family member of mine, was a photographer and needed help.

So, I volunteered to assist at a wedding, and for me, something was awakened. It was like walking into a storybook. The wedding was held a castle-like venue. The bride was breathless, it was just so magical. I was bitten! I set my sites to learning how to become a photogapher too. I am the youngest in my family of 7, and not always taken seriously. I believe that when someone has a passion that is unresolved, they wonder, and yes, I did different things and just wasnt satisfied. But whatever I did, I did to the best of my ability, my parents instilled a strong work ethic in me.In fact, she went on to become a celebrated photographer, photographing celebrities, winning awards, and teaching photography all over the world. And I was so fortunate, my sister-in-law, the photographer, whom I ended up learning from, is a gifted and talented photographer.

I was hooked, and I too, was gifted with a passion and an eye for photography. While my journey stayed close to home, in Tennessee, through the years, I earned many accolades and awards internationally and locally. I enjoyed the celebration of the wedding day, and loved all the clients I met and many places we traveled to locally and nationally.

And after so many years, I decided to take a break to be at home with my family, and my special needs son. My family and I treasure the years we had with him. We will miss him, and we know that we gave him a full life, and he gave us so much, his love.

I have been doing photography even while taking a break, its in my dna and I embrace it. And while I specialized in weddings for many years, after taking a break, life with son gave me such a different perspective, on how precious our family is and the time we have together. I now specialize in family and portrait photography, and lifestyle branding photography.

The family unit is so important to me, and I want to help you create beautiful images of your family.

I tried to leave the world of photography during my break, I went back to finish my degree in Business Management, obtaining my Bachelor's degree, and getting certified in Project Management, earning an extremely high score in my final exam. You have to understand, this was an impossible day, I did not sleep, I was 30 minutes late to the exam, and forgot my reading glasses. After taking the class, I informed the facilitator to reschedule me for the next test. Assuming I didnt pass, she asked why I wanted to do that when I had passed the exam. And then she informed me that I had scored the highest she had seen in years. I cried out of sheer exhaustion, joy and a bit of disbelief.

Also, during covid-19, when we were in lock down, I ventured into two other fields. I signed up for a class in coding, software programming, and wanted to enhance my knowledge, andlearn about gardening. I had learned gardening from my mother when I was young, but wanted to perfect my knowledge and skills. Well, having earned my certification in full stack web development,and I was asked to apply and join the team to teach coding. And I loved it! My first university that I taught at was Johns Hopkins University. I was also invited to join the instructional teams that were teaching coding for United Kingdom/Great Britain. I have enjoyed it these last five years, and now have decided to focus on my true passions, photography and gardening. Crazy right? God has taken me on many journeys, but I always came back to photography or photography kept finding me.

I believe in humans, in people of any age. I believe in life, and that we are all capable of doing anything we put our minds to. Nothing is unobtainable, and I especially want to share this message with young girls.

So you have learned a little bit about me. I have a big heart for you, and I know that I can help you. Most people are not crazy about how they look in pictures. I will show you that my style is easy, and I know how to capture you to bring forth your gorgeous you, your essence.

My thanks, and praise to all who have supported me, but especially to God above. For it is HE who has made me who I am today, and gifted me this wondrous skill of being a gifted photographer.

And the gardening.... as mentioned above..where does this come in? Well, that's my other business. Southern Kitchen Garden. People have always asked me if I could help them with their gardening needs, and I love it! And a friend told me, "I think its time you do that as a business!"
